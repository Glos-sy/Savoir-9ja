Here’s a list of popular and exciting events across different Nigerian cities that tourists would love to experience. These events showcase Nigeria’s rich culture, music, history, and traditions.

Lagos State (Nigeria's Entertainment & Cultural Hub)
  1. Lagos Carnival - April (Easter Period)
  2. Felabration (Afrobeat Festival) - October (Week of Fela Kuti's Birthday)
  3. Eyo Festival - No Fixed Date (Held for Special Occasions)
  4. GTCO Food & Drink Festival - April/May
  5. Lagos Fashion Week - October
Lagos State (Nigeria's Entertainment & Cultural Hub)
  1. Abuja Carnival - November
  2. Jabi Boat Festival - December
  3. Eid Festivals - Varies Based on Islamic Calendar
  4. Abuja International Film Festival - October
  5. Made in Nigeria Expo - March
Calabar (Home of Africa's Biggest Street Carnival)
  1. Calabar Carnival - December (All Month Long)
  2. Leboku New Yam Festival - August
  3. Calabar Boat Regatta - December
Kano (Northern Cultural Heritage)
  1. Durbar Festival - Held after Eid-El-Fitr & Eid-El-Kabir
  2. Kano International Trade Fair - November
Ogun State
  1. Jude Oba Festival - Third Day After Eid-El-Kabir
  2. Lisabi Festival (Egba Heritage Festival) - March
