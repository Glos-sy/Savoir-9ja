Safety tips to consider

Your safety is our priority. Explore our essential safety tips to ensure a secure and enjoyable visit while experiencing all that Nigeria has to offer.

Stay Weather Wise

Nigeria’s tropical climate can bring intense heat or heavy rains. Check the weather forecast before heading out and dress appropriately to stay comfortable

Emergency Contacts

  • Police: 112 or 199

    • Also, have your country’s embassy or consulate details available in case of emergencies.

    Transportation

    Stick to registered taxis or ride-hailing services like Uber and Bolt. Avoid unregulated transport options like okadas (motorbikes) or danfos (minibuses) for safety reasons.

    Protect your Health

    • Vaccinations: Check if you need shots for yellow fever, malaria prevention, or routine vaccines before traveling

    Avoid Scams & Fraud

    • Stick to official tour guides and negotiate prices beforehand.
    • Avoid making sensitive transactions over public Wi-Fi and beware of phishing scams.

    Stay in Secured Areas

    • Book hotels in well-secured, tourist-friendly areas.
    • Ask locals or hotel staff about safe zones and areas to avoid, especially at night.

    What should I know about local laws ?

    Understanding local laws is crucial for a safe trip. In Nigeria, customs may differ significantly from what you are used to. Always respect local traditions, and be aware of regulations regarding photography, public conduct, and dress codes to avoid misunderstandings.

    Are the specific areas to avoid ?

    While Nigeria is a beautiful country, some areas may have higher safety risks. It’s advisable to stay informed about which regions are recommended for tourists and to avoid areas with known safety concerns. Always consult with local authorities or guides for the most up-to-date information.

    How can I stay connected in Nigeria ?

    Staying connected is essential for safety. Consider purchasing a local SIM card for your phone upon arrival. This will allow you to access maps, communicate with locals, and stay updated on any safety alerts during your travels.
