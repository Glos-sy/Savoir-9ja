Lagos

Lagos, Nigeria, is a multilingual city with English as the official language, used for government, education, and business. However, the most widely spoken local language is Yoruba, as Lagos is predominantly a Yoruba-speaking state. Other commonly spoken languages include Pidgin English, a simplified form of English mixed with local dialects, which serves as a lingua franca for people of diverse ethnic backgrounds. Due to Lagos' diverse population, languages such as Igbo, Hausa, and other Nigerian indigenous languages are also spoken by various communities.
